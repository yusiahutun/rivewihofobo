Are at-home laser hair removal devices safe?

The short answer is yes, though you’ll want to make sure your skin tone is compatible with your machine (we’ll discuss that in a bit). First, it might be helpful to talk about how these laser treatments actually work. To inhibit growth, most devices rely on IPL (Intense Pulsed Light), which starts by detecting the pigment in your hair, then “zaps” it with heat to destroy the follicle. It’s only effective when done during the hair’s growth stage, which is why multiple treatments are often necessary. Sometimes, the results are permanent, but in many cases where hair grows back, it’s often finer and less visible than it was prior to the treatment. Before purchasing an at-home laser hair removal machine, keep in mind that light hair colors, like blonde, red, gray, and white, don’t absorb light as well as dark hair.

As mentioned before, you’ll want to consider your skin tone and hair color to decide if an at-home laser hair removal machine will be safe and effective for you. These devices work best when there is an obvious contrast between skin and hair colors, so they tend to favor light skin tones with dark hair. Unfortunately, many at-home laser machines are not safe for use on dark skin tones, as there is a risk of burning, but there are some products on the market that are designed to work with all skin tones (like this FDA-cleared device by Iluminage). As an added precaution, this Remington model has a built-in skin tone tester, and comes with a 90-day money back guarantee if you don’t think it’s a fit. If you’re concerned about the safety of using one of these machines with your skin tone, you can also opt for a professional laser treatment.

Another safety issue to keep in mind is the possibility of skin irritation, though the Mayo Clinic says that this side effect typically disappears in several hours. And, according to the Cleveland Clinic, you’ll want to avoid using the device on sensitive spots, such as tattooed areas, moles, genitals, and near your eyes. If you want to play it extra safe, consider going with an FDA-approved at-home laser hair removal machine.

Which laser for hair removal is the best?

There are many types of at-home laser hair removal machines, so the best one for you is the one that is safe to use with your skin tone and effectively targets the hair you want removed. Some devices are designed to treat smaller areas (we like this popular model from Tria for its precision), while others provide really good coverage for your entire body, like the Remington IPL6500QFB iLight Hair Removal System. There are also great affordable options, including the Fasbruy At-Home Laser Hair Remover (costs less than $100!), and we love this portable device for on-the-go grooming.

Something else to be on the lookout for are laser hair removal machines that have lots of “flashes,” so you’ll be able to zap unwanted hair away for years. This one by DEESS impressively comes with unlimited flashes, making it a great investment. In fact, many at-home laser hair removers don’t cost more than $500, which is about what you’d pay for just one professional laser treatment!

Once you’ve narrowed it down, you’ll be able to start targeting whichever areas have unwanted hair. After just a few treatments, you might start busting out those cute shorts and summer sundresses!
