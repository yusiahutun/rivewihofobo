    How to Choose the Best Redken Shampoo?

    Choosing the best Redken shampoo for your hair may feel overwhelming, given the wide variety of hair products offered by the brand. But you don't have to worry! Because we are here with our complete guide on what to look for when buying shampoo from Redken. This will help you make an informed choice. 

    Here are some aspects you must carefully consider before going shopping for a Redken shampoo at Target:

    Hair Type

    First of all, consider your hair type and scalp type, whether they are normal, oily, or dry. And yes, they can be different. You can have an oily scalp with dry hair ends and vice versa. Determining this will help you narrow down your choices.

    Hair Texture

    You must also consider your hair texture before picking up a Redken shampoo at Walmart, or any other place. Opt for a shampoo based on whether your hair is straight, wavy, curly, coily, or kinky. Also, consider if they are thin, thick, or fine. 

    Hair Concerns

    Different hair concerns require different ingredients, textures, and formulas. So, you must determine the specific hair issues you face, like dryness, dandruff, thinning hair, frizz, excess oil, dullness, rough texture, split ends, and hair damage, to name a few. Opt for a Redken shampoo that specifically targets your primary hair concerns.

    Ingredients

    Examine the ingredients list and make sure it doesn't contain anything that may cause allergies or sensitivity. You must look for nourishing ingredients like plant proteins, vitamins, amino acids, ceramides, peptides, arginine, coconut milk, argan oil, panthenol, glycerin, lactic acid, salicylic acid, and moisture complex.

    Formula

    Redken shampoos are gentle and have a pH-balanced formula. However, there are still some things about it that you must consider. If you have a dry scalp, opt for a moisturizing formulation and get your hands on a clarifying shampoo if you have oily hair type to get clean hair. Similarly, there are different formulations for damaged hair and color-treated hair for you to choose from.

    Budget 

    Redken shampoos can be quite expensive so it becomes a matter of investing in your hair. Set aside an amount of money you are willing to spend on shampoo for salon-like smooth hair at home.

    Consult a Professional

    If you are still confused amidst the sea of Redken shampoos, consult a board-certified dermatologist or a hair care professional for their opinions and recommendations. They can guide you to products that address your specific hair needs as they have a better idea about your hair and scalp conditions.

    Read Customer Reviews

    Don't forget to read customer reviews and feedback on various online platforms from people who share similar hair types, textures, and concerns. It will give you insights into the real-life efficacy and performance of the Redken shampoo you plan to buy.

    FAQ's

    Are Redken shampoos safe for color-treated hair?

    There are specific Redken shampoos that can be used for color-treated hair like the Redken Color Extend Magnetics Shampoo.

    Can Redken shampoos help with hair loss or thinning?

    Yes, Redken shampoos can help with hair loss and thinning. They improve hair and scalp health, giving way to healthy hair growth. They also reduce hair breakage and boost volume. You can opt for a shampoo like the Redken Brews Thickening Shampoo.

    Do hairdressers use Redken?

    Yes, hairdressers use Redken. In fact, it is one of the most loved hair care brands by hair stylists and hairdressers across the world.

    What is the Redken controversy?

    The Redken controversy occurred in 2022 when a class-action lawsuit was filed against the brand that claimed the presence of benzene, a cancer-causing ingredient, in their dry shampoo. It was outrageous especially because benzene was not specified as an ingredient on the product label. However, the lawsuit hasn’t ended yet.

    Should you use Redken every day?

    Some Redken shampoos are very good for everyday use. However, shampooing every day is not advisable. The frequency of your hair wash must depend on how dirty or greasy your hair gets.
