How do you use a heat protectant?

Hearst Owned

One thing to note: a little goes a long way. Nuriel Abramov, hairstylist and owner of Numi Salon, says to apply a pea-sized amount of product to hair as the first step in your routine post-shower. "Focus on your ends, which tends to be drier and where damage most occurs," Abramov says. Heat protectant can be used on wash days or any time you use a hot tool. Most formulas are best applied on damp hair, but read the instructions. “It is best to section your hair out and comb through to make sure the heat protectant is evenly distributed and applied throughout all of your hair," says Clark.

What should you look for in a heat protectant?

Hearst Owned

Silicones are notorious for creating a coating on the hair to protect it from heat. For a silicone-free formula with the same effect, look for ingredients like squalane to protect the hair. For weak and damaged hair, Abramov opts for keratin-infused products to help strengthen. For dry hair, look for oils like argan and coconut to moisturize and protect strands.
