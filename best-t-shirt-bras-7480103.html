The Best 15 Expert-Recommended T-Shirt Bras of 2024

The only closet staple more essential than a perfectly fitting white tee is the T-shirt bra that’s working its magic underneath. ‘[It’s] the foundation of any outfit,” says Iris Clarke, expert bra fitter and founder of Iris Lingerie. Unlike their lacy, embellished counterparts, what sets a T-shirt bra apart is that they’re meant to be undetectable, featuring “smooth, rounded cups with no seams at all,” says Clarke, bringing clean lines, natural lift, and that “can’t even feel it” comfort to your everyday style.

Not just for slipping under your favorite T-shirt, this versatile bra style can pair with almost anything in your wardrobe, from your work button-up to your night-on-the-town tank top. But with seemingly endless options, finding the right one to fit your body can be a challenge. We asked Clarke and two other bra experts to reveal their favorite T-shirt bras to suit every bust, body, and style. From the wired to the wireless, the lightweight to the heavy-duty, here are their top picks.

What to Keep in Mind

Style

There’s no shortage of style options when it comes to T-shirt bras, whether you’re looking for moderate or full coverage, lined or unlined, wired or wireless. Most T-shirt bras feature molded cups, “meaning that the bra keeps its shape whether or not there’s something in it, as opposed to a soft cup bra that’s flat when there are no boobs in them,” says Kaplan. This gives the bra its smooth, undetectable appearance.

Beyond that, it’s all a matter of personal preference. “Wire is what gives shape,” says Kaplan. “It contains the breast tissue in a nice little package,” but not everybody finds it comfortable, and wireless bras can provide a rounded, lifted shape without requiring stiff underwire. Since the majority of a bra’s support comes from the band around the ribcage, she adds, it’s important to seek out thick, sturdy bands to mimic the support of an underwire. Additionally, look for adjustable straps to ensure a custom fit, and for even more versatility, look for bras with convertible straps. Padded straps can be a bonus for added comfort, especially for those with a fuller bust.

Material

Most T-shirt bras are made with smooth fabrics that have some stretch, such as jersey, stretch, like nylon, polyester, cotton, or modal blended with spandex or elastane. They range from lightweight spacer fabric to more armor-like construction, but the one thing they have in common is that the material molds over the cup shape without needing seams or darts, creating a smooth appearance.

“Remember, the garment is closest to your skin so it should not itch or irritate you,” says Silberstein. That’s not to say that lace or other textured fabrics are out altogether, though. “People don't typically recognize a T-shirt bra to have lace, but it absolutely can,” she says. “As long as it's not lace on the cups. If it’s on the band, it’s pretty close to invisible and can work for sexier date night or to dress up your everyday looks.” Additionally, most t-shirt bras (but not all) have a light lining that helps give them shape while also providing light support.

Color

All the experts we spoke with agreed that having black and nude T-shirt bras in your rotation is essential. Nude bras disappear and are invisible under clothing, says Kaplan, unlike white bras which give off a “glow factor” under bright lights. “There's no such thing as custom color matching in the world of bras,” she says, “but try to get as close as you can to your skin tone.” Beyond that, adding a couple of colored or patterned options to match your wardrobe will give you more versatility and the opportunity to express your style, especially if you like your bra to stand out rather than blend in.

Your Questions, Answered

How do you measure yourself for a T-shirt bra?

Every bra is designed differently, so an in-person fitting is always going to give you the best results. But, if you’re sizing yourself up at home, “there are some measurements you can take to put you in the right neighborhood,” Kaplan says. To find your correct size, she advises measuring yourself around the top of the rib cage, right underneath the bust (where your bra band usually sits). If you land at an even number, that’s your band size. If you have an odd number, round up to the next even number. “If you like your bra snug, round down,” says Kaplan.

Then, measure the fullest part of your bust and subtract your band size from your bust measurement to find your cup size. “Every cup size is one inch away from the chest wall.” For example, if the difference is 0, it's an AA cup; 1 is an A cup; 2 is a B cup, and so on. Exact sizing and fit vary by brand, and once you find your size, it’s trial and error until you find a particular style that works best on your body. 

How should a T-shirt bra fit?

A T-shirt bra should provide subtle lift and shaping while still remaining comfortable enough to wear throughout the day. “Most of the issues women have with their bras is that they don't fit well — they’re not the right size or style for their body,” says Kaplan. “You want the cup of the bra to start back under your armpit because that's where your breasts start. And like with any bra, you want your breast to be sitting in the cup.” The band should be in the same place in the back as it is in the front, and the bridge of the bra (the centerpiece) should be sitting flat against your chest wall in between your breasts. “If it's sticking away, that means the cup is too small,” she explains. “There shouldn't be any bulging or spilling over, and if your bra is gapping, you're not filling it out on top.”

Even the perfect-fitting bra requires a little fiddling to get everything sitting just right. “You can't just slap your bra on; you’ve got to do some adjusting,” she adds, pointing out a  move bra fitters call the “swoop and scoop,” which involves leaning forward and “bringing each breast to the center of the bra cup and up,” so that they’re lifted and separated.

How long will a T-shirt bra last? 

Despite wishful thinking, a T-shirt bra won’t last forever. “A bra that is well taken care of will last six months to a year,” says Kaplan. “But, if you wear it every day and wash it frequently, especially in hot, sweaty weather, your body chemicals and washing detergents interact with the fibers more,” shortening your bra’s lifespan. That’s why rotation is key. “The more often you can rotate them, the longer each one will live."
